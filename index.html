    Robotic Hand Prosthetic

    Project date: September 2020 - Present

    Working with two professors and a group of seniors at my Institute, we have been working on developing a robotic hand prosthetic to assist a patient with Freidrich's Ataxia. The neurodegenerative disease prevents the patients from opening her hands from a clenched fist position and from partaking in ordinary everyday tasks such as holding silverware, using a smartphone, and getting dressed. The goal of the robotic prosthetic is to give her hand enough range of motion to allow her to perform her necessary daily tasks and lessen the strain on her body from attempting to open up her clenched fist.

    Running Water Lead-Detector

    Project date: August 2018 - May 2019

    I invented this device along with a partner in our senior year of High School in order to detect the levels of lead in our school's water fountains and sinks. It uses a screen-printed electrode connected to the LMP91000EVM Gas Sensor Development Board to send a current through the water and then sends the measured resistance back to the Raspberry Pi to calculate how much Lead is present. The amount of lead present is then outputted to the LCD screen for the user to see. The device uses an algorithm we created in Python on our Raspberry Pi to measure the prescence of lead down to the parts-per-million. We presented this project to a panel of professional engineers throughout the design process to receive feedback on what could be improved.
